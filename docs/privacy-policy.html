Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 21, 2026

Currents Fishing App ("Currents", "we", "our") is developed by Aidan McConnon. This policy explains how the app handles your data.

Summary

Currents is an offline-first app. Your data stays on your device. We do not collect, transmit, or sell your personal information.

Data Stored on Your Device

All data you create in Currents — including catches, spots, gear, trips, and photos — is stored locally on your device in a SQLite database. This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly choose to back it up to iCloud.

Location Data

Currents requests access to your location to show your position on the map, provide local fishing forecasts, and tag catches with coordinates. Location data is stored only on your device and is never transmitted to any server. You can revoke location access at any time in iOS Settings.

Camera and Photos

Currents requests access to your camera and photo library so you can attach photos to catch entries. Photos are stored locally on your device. They are not uploaded or shared with any third party.

iCloud Backup

If you choose to use the iCloud backup feature, a copy of your database is stored in your personal iCloud Drive account. This is governed by Apple's iCloud terms and privacy policy. We do not have access to your iCloud data.

Analytics and Tracking

Currents does not include any analytics SDKs, tracking pixels, advertising frameworks, or crash reporting tools. We collect zero telemetry.

Third-Party Services

Currents may use Apple's WeatherKit to provide weather and forecast data. These requests are handled by Apple and are subject to Apple's Privacy Policy. No personally identifiable information is sent with these requests.

Children's Privacy

Currents does not knowingly collect any personal information from children. Since no data is collected from any user, the app is safe for use by all ages.

Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a new "Last updated" date.
